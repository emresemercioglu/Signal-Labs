### FAQ Section (400 words)

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How does CIx track competitor product features?**

A: CIx monitors competitor websites, product pages, documentation, release notes, changelog, demo videos, and user reviews. We continuously catalog features, analyze capabilities, and track changes over time. For technical products, we also monitor engineering blogs, technical documentation, and API references.

**Q: Can CIx predict what competitors will build next?**

A: While we can't predict with certainty, CIx identifies signals that indicate roadmap direction: beta features, hiring patterns, technical blog posts, patent filings, partnership announcements, and customer feature requests on review sites. These signals help you anticipate competitive moves.

**Q: Does CIx help with UI/UX competitive analysis?**

A: Yes. CIx includes UI/UX competitive intelligence: design patterns, user experience approaches, interface decisions, and usability comparisons. Product and design teams use this to benchmark experiences and identify differentiation opportunities.

**Q: How technical is the competitive intelligence?**

A: CIx adapts to your technical depth needs. Product managers get feature-level intelligence. Engineering leaders get architectural and technical capability intelligence. You control the technical depth.

**Q: Can we track competitor beta programs and early releases?**

A: Yes. CIx monitors public beta programs, early access programs, and preview features. Get early visibility into what competitors are testing before general availability.

**Q: How does CIx help with build vs. buy decisions?**

A: CIx provides intelligence on how competitors approach similar capabilities: internal development, vendor solutions, open-source, or hybrid. Understand market approaches before making expensive, long-term technical decisions.

**Q: Can we integrate CIx with product management tools like Jira or Productboard?**

A: Yes. CIx integrates with major product management platforms. Enterprise customers can build custom integrations via API to embed competitive intelligence directly in product workflows.

**Q: How do I get my product team using CIx?**

A: Most product teams start with feature gap analysis and roadmap intelligence. PMs use CIx for roadmap planning, designers for UX benchmarking, engineering leaders for build vs. buy decisions. Adoption is gradual and role-specific.
