### Competitive Moat Assessment Framework (400 words)

**H2:** Assess Competitive Moats Systematically

Not all competitive advantages are moats. CIx helps assess moat strength and defensibility.

#### Types of Competitive Moats:

**Technology Moat**
- Proprietary technology or IP
- Technical complexity competitors can't replicate
- Patent protection
- Years of technical development lead

**Assessment Questions:**
- Can competitors replicate this technology?
- How long would replication take?
- Is IP protection meaningful?
- Is technical advantage sustainable?

---

**Data Moat**
- Proprietary data or unique data access
- Network effects from data
- Data that improves product over time

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is data proprietary or accessible to all?
- Do network effects create data advantages?
- Can competitors acquire similar data?
- Does data create lasting advantage?

---

**Network Effects Moat**
- Value increases with more users
- Multi-sided marketplace dynamics
- Strong network effects defensibility

**Assessment Questions:**
- Are network effects real and measurable?
- How strong are switching costs?
- Can competitors bootstrap competing networks?
- What's the minimum network size for value?

---

**Brand Moat**
- Strong brand recognition and trust
- Brand equity and customer loyalty
- Reputation that's hard to replicate

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is brand advantage measurable?
- How long to build comparable brand?
- Can competitors buy brand through marketing?
- Is brand defensible?

---

**Switching Costs Moat**
- High costs to switch to competitors
- Technical, financial, or operational lock-in
- Integration depth

**Assessment Questions:**
- How high are actual switching costs?
- Can competitors reduce switching friction?
- Are switching costs growing or shrinking?
- Do customers actually perceive switching costs?

---

**Scale & Cost Moat**
- Economies of scale
- Cost advantages from size
- Operational leverage

**Assessment Questions:**
- Does scale create meaningful advantage?
- Can well-funded competitors reach scale quickly?
- Are cost advantages sustainable?
- What's minimum efficient scale?

---

#### CIx Moat Assessment Output:

- Moat type identification
- Strength scoring (1-10)
- Defensibility assessment
- Competitive replicability timeline
- Risks to moat sustainability
- Recommendations for moat strengthening

### FAQ Section (400 words)

**H2:** Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How is CIx different from traditional market research for due diligence?**

A: Traditional market research provides point-in-time competitive analysis. CIx provides continuous intelligence: pre-investment for due diligence, post-investment for portfolio monitoring. Instead of one-time reports, you get ongoing competitive intelligence across all portfolio companies.

**Q: Can CIx replace full due diligence?**

A: No. CIx accelerates and enhances the competitive intelligence component of due diligence. You still need financial diligence, technical diligence, reference checks, and other due diligence streams. CIx makes competitive diligence faster, more comprehensive, and more systematic.

**Q: How long does competitive due diligence take with CIx?**

A: Comprehensive competitive landscape analysis: hours instead of weeks. Most investors complete competitive due diligence component in 1-2 days with CIx versus 1-2 weeks manually.

**Q: Can we share CIx with portfolio companies?**

A: Yes. Many VC firms provide CIx access to portfolio companies as value-add. Portfolio companies use CIx for their competitive intelligence, firm uses CIx for portfolio monitoring.

**Q: How does CIx help with market mapping for thesis development?**

A: CIx maps competitive landscapes across entire markets or sectors. Use for thesis development, sector analysis, and sourcing strategy. Understand competitive dynamics before diving into specific deals.

**Q: Can CIx identify red flags in competitive claims?**

A: Yes. CIx validates competitive claims against objective competitive data. Identify when "no real competitors" isn't accurate, when "2 years ahead" is overstated, or when moat claims don't hold up.

**Q: What about private/stealth competitors?**

A: CIx primarily tracks public competitive signals. Stealth competitors with no public presence are difficult to discover. However, CIx often identifies stealth companies through funding announcements, hiring patterns, and industry signals.

**Q: Can we customize CIx for our investment focus?**

A: Yes. Configure CIx to focus on your investment sectors, stage preferences, and competitive criteria. Customize for your firm's diligence framework.
